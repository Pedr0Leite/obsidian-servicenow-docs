# Technical Governance

**Technical Governance in ServiceNow: Comprehensive Guide for Case Study Analysis**

**1. Identifying a Technical Governance Case**

To determine if a case study revolves around Technical Governance, look for the following key elements:

•	**Decision-Making Frameworks**: Discussions on how technical decisions are made and approved.

•	**Standards and Policies**: Presence of coding standards, platform architecture guidelines, and governance policies.

•	**Compliance**: References to regulatory requirements or adherence to industry standards.

•	**Data Integrity and Security**: Governance over data accuracy, access control, and protection.

•	**Performance and Scalability**: Monitoring and enforcing system performance standards.

•	**Roles and Responsibilities**: Assignment of roles for maintenance, upgrades, and overall governance.

•	**Audit and Review Mechanisms**: Regular technical audits, reviews, or performance assessments.

**2. Role and Role Hierarchy in Technical Governance**

**Key Roles**

1.	**Platform Owner (Business/Technical Owner)**:

•	Defines governance policies.

•	Approves major platform changes.

•	Aligns platform strategy with business goals.

2.	**ServiceNow Architect**:

•	Designs and enforces technical standards and architecture.

•	Oversees implementation decisions to ensure compliance with governance.

•	Acts as the main advisor on scalability and performance.

3.	**Governance Lead (or Technical Governance Board)**:

•	Facilitates governance meetings.

•	Reviews and approves policies and standards.

•	Performs compliance audits and enforces governance frameworks.

4.	**Development Team Lead**:

•	Implements governance policies during development.

•	Ensures adherence to coding standards and guidelines.

•	Leads code reviews and enforces version control practices.

5.	**Change Advisory Board (CAB) Members**:

•	Evaluates risks and impacts of proposed changes.

•	Approves or rejects changes based on governance standards.

6.	**Compliance Manager**:

•	Oversees regulatory and security compliance.

•	Ensures data governance and audit requirements are met.

7.	**System Administrator**:

•	Implements access controls and monitors security.

•	Maintains instance health and enforces performance rules.

**3. Key Components of Technical Governance**

•	**Governance Model**: Centralized (one governing body), decentralized (team-level governance), or hybrid.

•	**Standards and Policies**:

•	Coding best practices.

•	Configuration vs. customization policies.

•	Naming conventions.

•	**Change Management**:

•	Risk assessments and rollback strategies.

•	Approval workflows via CAB.

•	**Risk and Compliance Management**:

•	Mitigation plans for integrations and upgrades.

•	Ensuring adherence to security standards.

•	**Performance and Monitoring**:

•	System uptime goals.

•	Periodic performance audits.

•	**Data Governance**:

•	Role-based access control.

•	Data retention and archiving policies.

•	**Automation**:

•	Use of flows, policies, and ATF to enforce governance.

**4. FAQ: Technical Governance in ServiceNow**

**Q1: What is Technical Governance in ServiceNow?**

**A:** It’s a framework of policies, standards, and practices to manage the platform’s development, performance, compliance, and security.

**Q2: Why is Technical Governance important?**

**A:** It ensures scalability, performance, and compliance while maintaining the platform’s integrity and reducing risks.

**Q3: What tools does ServiceNow offer for governance?**

**A:** Tools like Automated Test Framework (ATF), HealthScan, CMDB Health Dashboard, GRC, and Instance Security Center help enforce governance.

**Q4: Who is responsible for Technical Governance?**

**A:** A combination of roles, including the Platform Owner, Architect, CAB, and Development Lead, ensures governance is planned, executed, and maintained.

**Q5: How does governance support upgrades?**

**A:** Governance frameworks minimize risks by ensuring compatibility and adherence to standards before implementing upgrades.

**Q6: How does Technical Governance differ from IT Governance?**

**A:** Technical Governance focuses specifically on the technology and platform-level aspects of governance, whereas IT Governance is broader, encompassing overall IT processes and strategies.

**Q7: What are common challenges in Technical Governance?**

**A:** Challenges include lack of stakeholder buy-in, inadequate documentation, over-customization, and failure to update governance policies regularly.

**5. Case Study Analysis: Strategies**

•	**Analyze the Problem**: Identify governance gaps (e.g., missing standards, lack of monitoring).

•	**Review Stakeholder Roles**: Evaluate if roles are well-defined and actively contributing to governance.

•	**Assess Tools and Practices**: Are the right governance tools (ATF, HealthScan) in use?

•	**Identify Risks**: Highlight risks related to customizations, integrations, or non-compliance.

•	**Propose Improvements**:

•	Automate governance enforcement where possible.

•	Recommend regular performance and compliance audits.

**6. Tools and Resources for Effective Governance**

•	**ServiceNow Tools**:

•	ATF: Automate testing of customizations.

•	CMDB Health Dashboard: Monitor configuration accuracy.

•	GRC: Manage compliance risks.

•	Instance Security Center: Secure platform instances.

•	HealthScan: Audit instance performance and compliance.

•	**Third-Party Tools**:

•	Source control tools like GitHub or Jenkins.

•	Monitoring solutions for API performance.

**7. Best Practices for Technical Governance**

•	Adopt a **documented governance model** (centralized, decentralized, or hybrid).

•	Conduct regular **training sessions** for developers and administrators.

•	Automate **enforcement of policies** using ServiceNow tools.

•	Collaborate with stakeholders to ensure **buy-in and adherence**.

•	Regularly **review governance policies** to address evolving needs.
