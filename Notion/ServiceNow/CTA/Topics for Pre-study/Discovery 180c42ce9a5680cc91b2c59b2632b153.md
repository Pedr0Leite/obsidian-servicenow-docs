# Discovery

**ServiceNow Discovery: Comprehensive Guide for Case Study Analysis**

**1. Identifying a Discovery Case Study**

To determine if a case study focuses on ServiceNow Discovery, look for these indicators:

•	**CMDB Population and Maintenance**: Issues with missing, inaccurate, or outdated Configuration Items (CIs).

•	**Dependency Mapping**: Problems related to incomplete service maps or unclear application dependencies.

•	**Infrastructure Visibility**: Challenges in obtaining real-time visibility into IT assets and their relationships.

•	**Automation Needs**: Manual efforts for data updates, leading to inefficiencies or errors.

•	**Integration with Other Modules**: Discovery’s role in ITOM, ITSM, or ITAM processes.

•	**Cloud and Hybrid Environments**: Difficulty discovering assets in multi-cloud or hybrid infrastructures.

•	**Data Quality Issues**: Incomplete or inconsistent data in the CMDB, impacting incident, change, or problem management.

**2. Role and Role Hierarchy in Discovery**

**Key Roles**

1.	**Platform Owner (or CMDB/Discovery Owner)**:

•	Oversees Discovery’s role in maintaining the CMDB.

•	Aligns Discovery goals with organizational objectives.

2.	**Discovery Specialist**:

•	Configures and manages Discovery schedules and patterns.

•	Troubleshoots Discovery-related issues.

3.	**ServiceNow Architect**:

•	Designs Discovery solutions and ensures integration with other modules.

•	Advises on CI class mapping and dependencies.

4.	**CMDB Manager**:

•	Governs the CMDB, ensuring data from Discovery meets quality standards.

•	Validates CI relationships and dependencies.

5.	**Network Administrator**:

•	Provides network-level configurations (e.g., IP ranges, SNMP credentials) for Discovery.

•	Troubleshoots communication issues between Discovery probes and devices.

6.	**Cloud Architect**:

•	Ensures Discovery supports cloud platforms like AWS, Azure, and GCP.

•	Configures cloud discovery connectors for accurate data collection.

7.	**ITOM Practitioner**:

•	Utilizes Discovery data for event correlation, monitoring, and service mapping.

8.	**Service Owners**:

•	Define requirements for accurate representation of their services in the CMDB.

**3. Key Components of ServiceNow Discovery**

•	**Discovery Types**:

•	**Infrastructure Discovery**: Identifies physical and virtual assets like servers, routers, and databases.

•	**Cloud Discovery**: Discovers cloud resources (e.g., VMs, storage, load balancers) in AWS, Azure, and GCP.

•	**Application Dependency Mapping (ADM)**: Maps relationships between applications and infrastructure.

•	**Discovery Process**:

•	**Shazzam Probe**: Scans IP ranges to identify devices and services.

•	**Classification**: Identifies the device type (e.g., Windows Server, Cisco Router).

•	**Exploration**: Collects detailed attributes of devices and applications.

•	**Discovery Schedules**: Automates scans for specific IP ranges and intervals.

•	**MID Server**:

•	Acts as a communication bridge between ServiceNow and target devices.

•	Installed in customer environments to execute probes and scripts.

•	**Data Integration**:

•	Integrates with Service Mapping, Event Management, and CMDB Health for better insights.

**4. FAQ: ServiceNow Discovery**

**Q1: What is ServiceNow Discovery?**

**A:** It is a tool that automates the identification of IT assets and their relationships, populating the CMDB for better visibility and management.

**Q2: Why is Discovery critical for the CMDB?**

**A:** It ensures the CMDB remains accurate and up-to-date, which is essential for processes like incident, problem, change, and event management.

**Q3: What are common challenges with Discovery?**

**A:**

•	Incomplete or outdated IP range configurations.

•	Lack of appropriate MID Server placements.

•	Authentication failures for target devices.

•	Misclassification of CIs or incorrect dependencies.

**Q4: What types of environments can Discovery support?**

**A:** On-premises infrastructure, virtualized environments (e.g., VMware), and cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, GCP).

**Q5: How does Discovery differ from Service Mapping?**

**A:** Discovery focuses on discovering individual assets and their attributes, while Service Mapping identifies dependencies to create service maps.

**Q6: What is a MID Server, and why is it important?**

**A:** The MID Server is an on-premises component that facilitates secure communication between the ServiceNow instance and target devices for Discovery.

**Q7: How does Discovery integrate with ITSM and ITOM?**

**A:** Discovery populates the CMDB, enabling accurate incident and change management (ITSM) and event correlation (ITOM).

**5. Case Study Analysis: Strategies**

•	**Identify the Problem Statement**:

•	Are there gaps in CMDB data quality?

•	Are dependencies between applications and infrastructure missing?

•	Is there limited visibility into cloud resources?

•	**Evaluate Current Discovery Practices**:

•	Are IP ranges and credentials configured properly?

•	Is the MID Server placed in appropriate network zones?

•	Are Discovery schedules optimized for timely updates?

•	**Analyze Metrics**:

•	CMDB data accuracy, completeness, and compliance with governance policies.

•	Success rates of Discovery probes and patterns.

•	**Propose Solutions**:

•	Expand or refine IP ranges and credentials to improve asset discovery.

•	Deploy additional MID Servers to ensure coverage in all network segments.

•	Integrate with Service Mapping for better dependency visibility.

•	Automate CMDB data validation using CMDB Health Dashboard.

**6. Tools and Resources for Discovery**

•	**ServiceNow Tools**:

•	**Discovery Patterns**: For detailed attribute collection and classification.

•	**MID Server Dashboard**: Monitors MID Server health and performance.

•	**CMDB Health Dashboard**: Assesses data quality, compliance, and completeness.

•	**Cloud Discovery Connectors**: For discovering AWS, Azure, and GCP assets.

•	**Third-Party Tools**:

•	Integration with monitoring tools like SolarWinds or Nagios for richer data.

•	Network management solutions for troubleshooting connectivity issues.

**7. Best Practices for Discovery Implementation**

•	**Plan for Coverage**:

•	Map network zones and deploy MID Servers strategically.

•	Regularly update IP ranges and credentials.

•	**Optimize Discovery Schedules**:

•	Avoid overlapping schedules to prevent redundant scans.

•	Adjust intervals based on asset change frequency.

•	**Ensure Data Quality**:

•	Use CMDB Health Dashboard to identify and resolve discrepancies.

•	Define policies for CI de-duplication and retirement.

•	**Enable Collaboration**:

•	Work with network and cloud teams for configuration support.

•	Engage service owners to validate discovered data.

•	**Leverage Automation**:

•	Automate recurring tasks like credential updates or decommissioning stale CIs.
