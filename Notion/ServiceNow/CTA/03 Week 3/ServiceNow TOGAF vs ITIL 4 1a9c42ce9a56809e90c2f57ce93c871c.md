# ServiceNow: TOGAF vs. ITIL 4

Both **TOGAF** and **ITIL 4** are widely used frameworks, but they serve different purposes within **ServiceNow implementations**.

| **Aspect** | **TOGAF (The Open Group Architecture Framework)** | **ITIL 4 (IT Infrastructure Library)** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Focus** | Enterprise Architecture (EA) | IT Service Management (ITSM) |
| **Purpose** | Provides a **structured methodology** for designing, planning, implementing, and governing enterprise IT architecture. | Defines **best practices for IT service delivery and management**, ensuring efficiency and value to the business. |
| **Key Components** | - Architecture Development Method (ADM)  - Business, Data, Application, and Technology (BDAT) domains  - Governance & risk management | - Service Value System (SVS)  - Four Dimensions of Service Management  - ITIL 4 Practices (e.g., Change, Incident, Problem, Service Management) |
| **ServiceNow Relevance** | Helps define **instance strategy, platform governance, and integration architecture**. | Helps structure **ITSM processes** like Incident, Change, Problem, and Service Request Management. |
| **Best Use Case** | Used when **designing a full enterprise architecture** for ServiceNow adoption across business units and integrating with legacy systems. | Used when **optimizing IT service management** and aligning processes with business goals using ServiceNow ITSM. |

### **How They Work Together in ServiceNow**

- **TOGAF defines the enterprise architecture** that supports ServiceNow’s implementation across IT and business units.
- **ITIL 4 guides the operational processes** within ServiceNow’s ITSM, ensuring smooth service delivery.
- **Example**: TOGAF helps determine **how ServiceNow integrates** with existing IT systems, while ITIL 4 ensures **incident resolution, change approvals, and service continuity**.
