# Customer Service Management (CSM)

**I. Introduction to Customer Service Management (CSM)**

**Definition**

•	**Customer Service Management (CSM)** is a suite of applications in ServiceNow designed to streamline and improve the customer service process. It focuses on providing superior service to customers, improving satisfaction, and resolving issues efficiently across various channels (web, phone, email, etc.).

•	CSM connects customers with the right resources within an organization, providing a 360-degree view of the customer journey.

**Key Benefits of CSM**

•	**Improved Customer Experience**: Centralized data enables customer service teams to provide quicker and more personalized responses.

•	**Automated Case Management**: Automation reduces manual effort in tracking and managing cases, allowing service teams to focus on high-priority tasks.

•	**Proactive Issue Resolution**: Predictive analytics and insights allow teams to address issues before they become problems, enhancing customer satisfaction.

•	**Collaboration**: Provides collaborative tools for customer service agents, IT teams, and other departments to work together on case resolution.

**Use Cases for CSM**

•	**Incident Management**: Handling customer-reported issues and incidents.

•	**Request Fulfillment**: Managing requests for services, products, or information.

•	**Field Service Management**: Assigning and tracking work orders for field service teams.

•	**Knowledge Management**: Delivering self-service solutions through knowledge articles and FAQs.

**II. Key Components of CSM**

1.	**Cases**:

•	Cases are central to CSM. They represent customer issues or inquiries that need resolution. A case can be opened through multiple channels (web, phone, email, etc.) and follows a lifecycle that includes categorization, prioritization, resolution, and closure.

•	**Case Management** helps to track and resolve customer issues efficiently, providing clear workflows and ownership.

2.	**Customer Service Catalog**:

•	Similar to a service catalog for IT, the **Customer Service Catalog** enables the organization to provide pre-defined services or requests that customers can choose from.

•	Examples include warranty claims, product replacements, service requests, etc.

3.	**Knowledge Management**:

•	**Knowledge Articles** allow customers to find solutions to common problems without needing to interact with support agents. These articles are accessible via the **Customer Service Portal**.

•	**Knowledge Base** helps reduce case volume by empowering customers to resolve issues independently.

4.	**Service Level Agreements (SLAs)**:

•	SLAs define the expected timelines for case resolution. CSM includes tools to monitor and enforce SLAs, ensuring that customers receive timely responses and resolutions.

•	SLAs can be based on various metrics such as response time, resolution time, or escalations.

5.	**Omnichannel Support**:

•	**Omnichannel** provides customer service agents with a single interface to handle interactions from multiple channels, including chat, email, phone, and social media.

•	**Virtual Agent** and **Live Chat** are key components for offering real-time support and automation.

6.	**Field Service Management (FSM)**:

•	**FSM** extends CSM by enabling the organization to manage and track field service tasks. This includes scheduling, dispatching, and tracking work orders for on-site service teams.

•	FSM can be integrated with other ServiceNow applications like Incident Management and Change Management.

7.	**Customer Portal**:

•	A **Self-Service Portal** provides customers with access to case tracking, knowledge articles, FAQs, and service requests. It enhances customer satisfaction by enabling users to get answers without contacting support.

•	The portal also supports features like proactive notifications, allowing customers to track the progress of their cases or requests in real time.

8.	**Reporting & Analytics**:

•	**Reports & Dashboards** offer insights into customer service performance, such as case resolution times, customer satisfaction scores, and SLA compliance.

•	**Performance Analytics** can help identify trends, bottlenecks, and opportunities for process improvements.

**III. Creating and Configuring CSM Applications**

1.	**Designing Case Management Workflows**:

•	**Define Case Types**: Categorize cases based on different types of customer interactions (e.g., incident, service request, product inquiry).

•	**Case Assignment**: Automate case assignments to the appropriate support agents or teams based on predefined rules (e.g., SLA, priority, or expertise).

•	**Case Routing**: Define conditions for routing cases to the right support group or individual.

2.	**Service Catalog Configuration**:

•	Create and define services that are available to customers. Services might include product requests, service renewals, technical assistance, etc.

•	**Catalog Items**: Configure catalog items with forms that collect the necessary information to process requests, such as customer details or issue descriptions.

3.	**Omnichannel Configuration**:

•	Configure **Omnichannel Support** to enable agents to interact with customers across multiple channels (email, chat, phone, etc.).

•	Implement **Virtual Agent** to handle repetitive questions or tasks, reducing the workload of customer service agents.

•	Use **Live Chat** to provide real-time support directly within the portal.

4.	**Defining SLAs**:

•	Establish SLAs for various case types, ensuring that each case is resolved within the agreed-upon time frame.

•	Use **SLA Definitions** to specify conditions for when an SLA starts, pauses, and ends.

•	Set up **SLA Reports** and **Performance Analytics** to track adherence to SLAs.

5.	**Customer Portal Configuration**:

•	Customize the **Customer Service Portal** to align with the company’s branding and service offerings.

•	Ensure that the portal includes features such as case tracking, knowledge articles, and a request form for common service requests.

**IV. Best Practices for CSM Implementation**

1.	**Design an Intuitive and User-Friendly Portal**:

•	Ensure the **Customer Portal** is simple to navigate, with clear access to case tracking, knowledge base, and service requests.

•	Implement self-service options like knowledge articles to empower customers to resolve issues without contacting support.

2.	**Automate Case Management**:

•	Leverage automation tools like **Business Rules** and **Flow Designer** to streamline case management workflows (e.g., automatic case creation, case routing, or escalations).

•	Use **Event Management** for real-time monitoring and automated alerts in case of system or product issues.

3.	**Ensure Proper Integration with Other ServiceNow Modules**:

•	Integrate CSM with modules such as **Incident Management**, **Problem Management**, **Change Management**, and **Knowledge Management** for a more seamless support process.

•	Consider integrating CSM with **Field Service Management (FSM)** to address field-related issues.

4.	**Optimize for Omnichannel**:

•	Ensure that agents have a **single pane of glass** to manage interactions from multiple channels.

•	Implement **Virtual Agents** and **Chatbots** to handle repetitive queries and case initiation, freeing up agents to focus on complex tasks.

5.	**Monitor Performance and Continuously Improve**:

•	Regularly review **Performance Analytics** reports to assess key performance indicators (KPIs) like case resolution time, SLA adherence, and customer satisfaction.

•	Use feedback from customers and agents to continuously improve service workflows and the overall customer experience.

6.	**Proactive Customer Service**:

•	Utilize predictive analytics to identify potential service issues before they affect customers (e.g., outages, delays).

•	Set up automated notifications and proactive alerts to inform customers of status changes in their cases or requests.

**V. Handling CSM Challenges**

1.	**Data Management and Integration**:

•	Integrate data from various systems (CRM, ERP, external ticketing systems) to provide a holistic view of customer issues.

•	Use **IntegrationHub** for seamless integrations with external applications.

2.	**Managing Multiple Channels**:

•	Providing consistent support across multiple communication channels can be challenging. Ensure you have the right tools in place to track interactions across channels (e.g., **Omnichannel** or **Unified Agent Workspace**).

•	Maintain a **centralized case repository** to ensure that all channels reference the same case information.

3.	**Handling SLAs and Expectations**:

•	Define clear and realistic SLAs based on historical data and industry standards.

•	Keep customers informed about any delays or challenges in meeting SLAs to maintain satisfaction.

4.	**Employee Training and Adoption**:

•	Invest in training for customer service agents on how to use the CSM tools effectively.

•	Use **Agent Assist** features to guide agents during case resolution, reducing training time and error rates.

**VI. Publishing and Deployment of CSM Applications**

1.	**User Acceptance Testing (UAT)**:

•	Before deploying CSM to a production environment, conduct thorough **User Acceptance Testing** (UAT) to ensure all workflows and processes meet the business requirements.

2.	**Change Management**:

•	Use **Change Management** practices to ensure that any modifications to the CSM process or configurations are tested and deployed systematically.

3.	**Continuous Improvement**:

•	After deploying CSM, continuously monitor customer service performance. Collect feedback from customers and agents to make improvements.

•	Update knowledge articles regularly based on common customer issues and frequently asked questions.
