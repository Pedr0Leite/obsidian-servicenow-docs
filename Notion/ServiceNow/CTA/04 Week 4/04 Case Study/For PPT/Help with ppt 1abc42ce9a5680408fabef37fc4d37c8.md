# **Slide 2: Executive Summary**

- **Objective:** Establish **technical governance** for release and instance strategy.
- **Key Takeaways:**
    - Implement **scalable governance model** for **ITSM, ITOM, CSM, and HR**.
    - Define **incremental release strategy** (avoiding a big bang approach).
    - Select an **agile framework** for **multi-team implementations**.

---

# **Slide 3: The Need for Strong Technical Governance**

### **Current Challenges at Sovereign Bank**

- **Siloed decision-making** → Misalignment between business and IT.
- **Business demands platform customization** instead of adjusting processes.
- **Future expansions (Legal Service Delivery, Financial Services Ops) add demand.**
- **Multiple independent scrum teams** → Need for structured governance.

### **Business Impact Without Governance**

- **Uncontrolled platform changes** → Upgrade and scalability risks.
- **Inefficient releases** → No clear roadmap, leading to delays.
- **Scalability issues** → Future capabilities not accounted for.

---

# **Slide 4: Technical Governance Board: Goals & Priorities**

### **Goals of the Technical Governance Board**

1. **Standardize platform decisions** → Ensure IT & business alignment.
2. **Define release & instance strategy** → Enable smooth upgrades (ServiceNow release +8 weeks).
3. **Enforce platform guardrails** → Prevent excessive customizations & ensure process-driven changes.
4. **Ensure scalability** → Support future ServiceNow modules & integrations.
5. **Maintain security & compliance** → Align governance with financial regulations.

### **Order of Priorities**

1. **Establish platform guidelines** (customization limits, data ownership rules).
2. **Define release strategy** (incremental rollouts, agile-driven sprints).
3. **Implement governance processes** (CAB for change approvals, instance controls).
4. **Support scaling & future capabilities** (Legal & Financial Services Ops readiness).
5. **Enforce upgrade compliance** (staying within +8 weeks of ServiceNow releases).

---

# **Slide 5: Incremental Release Strategy**

### **Recommended Approach**

✅ **Phased rollouts instead of a big bang:**

- **Phase 1 (ITSM, CMDB, HR Core)** → Standardize & migrate core processes.
- **Phase 2 (ITOM, CSM, Integrations)** → Automate & optimize operations.
- **Phase 3 (Advanced Capabilities)** → Expand to Legal, Financial Services Ops.

✅ **Instance Strategy:**

- **Production:** Stable release for live operations.
- **Sub-production instances:** Dev, Test, UAT for controlled change deployments.
- **Sandbox environments:** For innovation without impacting main releases.

**Visual Suggestion:** A Gantt chart showing phased rollout timelines and dependencies.

---

# **Slide 6: Avoiding a Burning Platform**

🚨 **Risks Without Governance:**

- **Uncontrolled customizations** → Blocking future upgrades.
- **Disorganized release cycles** → Teams operating in silos with no synchronization.
- **Security & compliance gaps** → Regulatory misalignment due to lack of oversight.

✅ **Best Practices to Avoid This:**

1. **Strict change control processes** → Enforce CAB review for major changes.
2. **Limit customizations** → Prioritize configuration over scripting.
3. **Align release strategy with ServiceNow’s upgrade cycle** (stay within +8 weeks).
4. **Monitor platform health** → Regular performance & security audits.

**Visual Suggestion:** A risk heatmap illustrating potential issues and mitigation strategies.

---

# **Slide 7: Selecting the Right Agile Framework**

### **Factors to Consider**

- **Multiple parallel implementations** → ITSM, ITOM, CSM, HR, and future capabilities.
- **Cross-team coordination needed** → Different scrum teams must align.
- **Need for flexibility & quick value delivery** → Incremental, scalable approach.

✅ **Recommended Agile Framework: SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework)**

- **Why?**
    - Supports **multiple teams working in parallel** on different modules.
    - Provides **centralized governance (Agile Release Train)** to align business & IT.
    - Enables **incremental value delivery through Program Increments (PIs)**.

**Other Options:**

- **LeSS (Large-Scale Scrum):** Good for fewer teams, but Sovereign Bank has multiple implementations.
- **Scrum of Scrums:** Works for coordinating independent teams, but lacks structured governance.

**Visual Suggestion:** A comparison chart of SAFe, LeSS, and Scrum of Scrums.

---

✅ **Immediate Next Steps:**

1. **Establish Technical Governance Board** and define priorities.
2. **Finalize incremental release & instance strategy**.
3. **Implement agile framework (SAFe) to support multiple scrum teams**.
4. **Define platform compliance policies** to avoid a burning platform.

✅ **Final Thought:**

- **By implementing structured governance, Sovereign Bank can ensure scalability, security, and efficiency in its ServiceNow rollout.**
